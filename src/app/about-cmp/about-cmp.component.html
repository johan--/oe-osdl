About the Oregon Spatial Data Library

Mission

The Oregon Spatial Data Library provides public access to reliable and up-to-date spatial data. The Oregon Spatial Data Library is a joint effort between the Department of Administrative Services Geospatial Enterprise Office and Oregon State University. Currently, hundreds of spatial datasets are accessible from the Oregon Spatial Data Library, including all of the statewide framework data available for Oregon. These datasets serve as base data for a variety of Geographic Information System (GIS) applications that support research, business and public services.


Audience

The targeted primary users for the Oregon Spatial Data Library are staff of agencies participating in the Oregon Geographic Information Council (OGIC), including Federal, State, and local governments and tribes. Secondary users include the general public, consultants, educators, and students.


How to Publish Data:

To publish data contact virtualoregon.support@oregonstate.edu.

Requests to upload framework data elements should go to Theresa Burcsu, DAS framework coordinator (theresa.burcsu@oregon.gov).

 

  • Collection Guidelines:

    ·   Data and metadata are in their final form.

    ·   Metadata complies with OSDL minimum metadata requirements.

    ·   All file names and attributes are well described and interpretable. Any acronyms used are defined in the metadata.

    ·   The data has potential value for other application areas or to support decision making.

    ·   The geographic extent is substantial (approximately the size of an average county or 4th field HUC (700 square miles)), or the extent covers a city.

     

  • Minimum Metadata Requirements:

    ·  Title (Overview>Item Description>Title)

    ·  Description/Abstract (Overview>Item Description>Description (Abstract))

    ·  Topic Categories/ISO Theme (Overview>Topics&Keywords>Topic Categories)

    ·  Metadata Contact (Metadata>Contacts>New Contact>New Contact Information (set role to “Originator”))

    ·  Publish Date (Overview>Citation>Dates>Published)
